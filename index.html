Prosthetics Wrist

Arduino/C/HyperTerminal/Solidworks

Made a prototype for computer controlled prospethic wrist
Abstract
For a more perfect wrist, one needs to emulate not only the rotational motion, but also the pivoting motion as well. This is what we decided to solve. Through my research, I have yet to discover a prosthetic wrist that can provide pivoting movements, only rotational. The major setback is creating a model that can move the full range of motion without interference. I decided to tackle this issue by connecting the hand to a ring on sliders that will allow the hand to turn side to side. A perpendicular motor that does the up and down rotation. With the axles on a hinge, it would be able to have full range of motion. By attaching wires to sides of the wrist that are controlled by motors when moved in conjunction with the ring will emulate the full range of motion necessary for an ideal wrist. With this project I hope to enhance the biotechnical field in prosthetic. And at the same time, more importantly, allow those who acquires a prosthetic wrist `to have a wrist move the way they want it to move not offset by limitations.

Prescription Bottle Cap

Solidworks/3D Printing

Made a prototype for a new prescription bottle cap
Abstract
The purpose of this project was to address the problem of prescription bottles. According to current FDA test, 15% of children ages 42 to 51 months were able to successfully open the traditional Push N’ Turn bill bottle. This shockingly high percentage is not acceptable. I tackled this problem two years ago in a prior Science Fair project. In that project I purposed a Lock and Key bottle cap to replace the Push N’ Turn cap. The designed featured a free spinning inner and outer layer. There is a triangular keyhole on the to upper layer for the user to access the inner layer. The inner layer had three notches that need to be lined up with the bottle rim to open. To align the notches a triangular key is provided. This year I provide a new design. In this new design the cap has three compressible buttons around the rim of the cap. To open the cap the user simply holds down the three buttons and lift. This design is a lot more efficient since it does away with the key and is a lot more elderly friendly. It design would be childproof because a toddlers hands are not big enough to hold down three button while lifting the cap as the same time.
